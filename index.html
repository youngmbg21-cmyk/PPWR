EU Packaging Regulation · Effective August 2026

The rules on packaging just changed.
Here's what your company needs to do.

A new EU law — called PPWR PPWR stands for Packaging & Packaging Waste Regulation (EU) 2025/40. It replaces an older 1994 directive and sets stricter rules on packaging across all EU and EEA markets. — applies to any business that places packaged products on the market in Europe. If your organisation uses boxes, bags, wrappers, bottles, or any kind of container, this regulation applies to you. This platform walks you through it — step by step, in plain language.

⏰
Enforcement starts August 12, 2026 — calculating… away.

After that date, packaging that doesn't comply can be pulled from shelves, rejected by retailers, or result in fines from national regulators. The best time to prepare is now.

♻️ Packaging Grades (A–E)

Under the new regulation, every piece of packaging gets a recyclability grade — A (best) to E (worst). Grade D and E packaging will be banned from 2030.

Use this lab to match your packaging materials against the grading criteria — and see how the grade affects your recycling fee.

🧮 Recycled Material Check

The regulation sets minimum targets for how much recycled material must be in plastic packaging — starting from 2030. Check whether your packaging meets the current and future requirements.

Also checks your packaging's "empty space ratio" — the regulation limits how much wasted space is allowed in a box or container.
